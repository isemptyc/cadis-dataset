# 1. Purpose

This document provides a structural, behavioral, and boundary-integrity
evaluation of the `co.admin v1.0.0` dataset under Cadis Runtime.

The Colombia dataset is scoped to administrative coverage derived from OSM
administrative levels 4 and 6 within the Natural Earth `CO` admin-0 boundary.
This scoped boundary is generated by `scripts/build_co_boundaries.py`.

Cadis does not modify geography. It enforces deterministic runtime policy over
the released dataset artifacts.

# 3. Scope

Dataset scope: Colombia administrative coverage derived from OSM admin levels
4 and 6 within the `CO` Natural Earth admin-0 boundary.

Scope builder:

- `scripts/build_co_boundaries.py`

Selection rule:

- Union OSM administrative relation areas at levels 4 and 6 whose
  representative point is covered by the Natural Earth `CO` boundary.

Generated scoped boundary:

- Bounding box: `[-81.7356633, -4.2280906, -66.8511118, 13.3945076]`
- Admin level counts used for scope: level 4 = `33`, level 6 = `1044`

This scope intentionally avoids treating broad Natural Earth coastal/water
coverage as inside-dataset administrative coverage when no OSM administrative
polygon is present.

# 11. Boundary Isolation Validation

Under stress testing with 10% forced outside-scope samples:

- No boundary leak failure was observed.
- Outside-scope samples resolved to `failed` or `offshore` as expected.
- Nearby/offshore handling did not create cross-border escalation in this run.

The initial full Natural Earth boundary evaluation produced one inside-labeled
empty-shape failure in coastal/water coverage. The tracked boundary builder was
therefore updated to use deterministic OSM administrative coverage within the
Natural Earth country boundary. The final evaluation above uses that scoped
boundary and passes fully.
